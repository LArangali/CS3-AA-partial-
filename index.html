Haikyuu!!

"Haikyu!! (ハイキュー!!, Haikyū!!, from the kanji 排球 "volleyball") is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Haruichi Furudate. It was serialized in Shueisha's shōnen manga magazine Weekly Shōnen Jump from February 2012 to July 2020, with its chapters collected in 45 tankōbon volumes. The story follows Shoyo Hinata, a boy determined to become a great volleyball player despite his small stature.


An anime television series adaptation produced by Production I.G, aired on MBS from April to September 2014, with 25 episodes. A second season aired from October 2015 to March 2016, with 25 episodes. A third season aired from October to December 2016, with 10 episodes. A fourth season was announced during the Jump Festa '19 and was released in two split cours from January to December 2020, with 25 episodes. The anime film series titled Haikyu!! Final, has been announced, which serves as the finale of the series.


Haikyuu!! centers on the Karasuno High School volleyball club, particularly its journey to regain its former glory. The story focuses on Shoyo Hinata, a short but highly athletic player who aspires to become a great volleyball player despite his stature. He joins Karasuno High's team and forms a dynamic duo with Tobio Kageyama, a highly skilled setter who was his rival in middle school. The series follows the team as they compete in various volleyball matches, aiming to qualify for nationals and face off against formidable opponents. It explores themes of teamwork, personal growth, determination, and the passion for the sport of volleyball.

Haikyuu!! is highly popular both in Japan and internationally. It has a dedicated fanbase and has received critical acclaim for its well-developed characters, engaging plot, and exciting volleyball action. The series has contributed to a resurgence in the popularity of sports anime and has inspired fans to take an interest in volleyball. It's known for its positive and motivational message about teamwork and perseverance. Haikyuu!! has won numerous awards, including the Shogakukan Manga Award and the Crunchyroll Anime Awards.

Karasuno High School (烏野高校, Karasuno Kōkō) is located in Miyagi Prefecture, Tōhoku region. The karasu part of the school name means 'crow'. The Karasuno volleyball team is nicknamed "Flightless Crows" and "Fallen Champs" since they were a powerhouse team that had fallen from grace; they are also called "Country Bumpkin Crows" by rival Nekoma captain Kuroo since they live in a rural area. According to Oikawa, Karasuno doesn't have a particular play style and instead opts to experiment with new styles. Karasuno has a long-standing rivalry with Nekoma High School, located in Tokyo, which is dubbed the "Battle at the Trash Heap" because crows and cats (Nekoma's mascot) are known to rummage through garbage. Within its own prefecture, its main rivals are Aoba Johsai High School, Date Tech High School, and Shiratorizawa Academy. Karasuno's banner is all black with the sole word "fly" in white. Its team colors are black and orange.
